Strong Passwords:
Create complex, unique passwords for each account using a mix of upper and lower case letters, numbers, and symbols. Change them regularly.
Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):
Enable 2FA wherever possible. This adds an extra layer of security by requiring a second verification step, such as a code from your phone.
Update Software:
Regularly update your operating system, apps, and antivirus software. Updates often contain critical security patches
Phishing Awareness:
Be cautious of unsolicited emails, links, or attachments. Don't share personal or financial information unless you're certain of the sender's legitimacy.
Secure Wi-Fi:
Use strong encryption for your Wi-Fi network and change the default router password.
Firewall Protection:
Activate a firewall to block unauthorized access to your network.
Data Backups:
Regularly back up your data to an external source to prevent loss from ransomware or hardware failures.
Privacy Settings:
Adjust privacy settings on social media and limit the information you share online.
Safe Browsing:
Use a reputable antivirus software and browser extensions to block malicious websites and downloads.
